Species of Moths

Acherontia atropos

The name Death's-head Hawkmoth refers to any one of the three moth species of the genus Acherontia (Acherontia atropos, Acherontia styx and Acherontia lachesis). The former species is found primarily in Europe, the latter two are Asian; most uses of the common name refer to the European species. These moths are easily distinguishable by the vaguely human skull-shaped pattern of markings on the thorax. All three species are fairly similar in size, coloration, and life cycle. Picture by MHNT.

Actias luna

The Luna moth is a lime-green, Nearctic Saturniid moth in the family Saturniidae, subfamily Saturniinae. It has a wingspan of up to 114 mm, making it one of the largest moths in North America.

Antheraea polyphemus

The polyphemus moth is a North American member of the family Saturniidae, the giant silk moths. Their average wingspan is 15 cm.

Attacus atlas

The Atlas moth is a large sturniid moth from the forests of Southeast Asia. Their wingspans are amongst the largest, reaching over 25 cm.

Biston betularia

The peppered moth is a temperate species of night-flying moth. They are famous for being used as an example of natural selection.

Callosamia promethea

The Promethea Silkmoth, is a member of the Saturniidae family of moths. Their wingspan measures up to 9.5 cm

Heniocha dyops

The Marbled Emperor, is a moth of the Saturniidae family. It is found in Angola, Kenya, Namibia, South Africa and Tanzania.

Hyalophora cecropia

The Cecropia moth has a wingspan of 160mm and is the largest moth native to North America.

Opodiphthera eucalypti

The Emperor gum moth, Opodiphthera eucalypti, is a species of moth in the family Saturniidae, that is native to Australia. This species was formerly placed in the genus Antheraea. Their wingspan reaches up to 150 mm.

Thysania agrippina

The white witch moth, Thysania agrippina, is the largest moth in the world. One Brazilian specimen has a wingspan of almost 30 cm!
